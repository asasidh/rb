Research & Discovery
⏱ 10 minutes
▼
💡 Facilitator: This stage maps the landscape. Give participants a few minutes to read the output, then discuss. Challenge the AI's assumptions with real-world experience.

Prompt

🎯 What to Look For

  • Are the personas realistic for this business?
  • Do the pain points match what the team actually experiences?
  • Are the success metrics measurable and achievable?
  • Anything the AI missed that the team knows from experience?

✏️ Team Discussion Questions

  • "Which persona feels the most pain today?"
  • "What workarounds are we already using that the AI didn't mention?"
  • "Are these the right KPIs, or are we measuring the wrong things?"
2
Root-Cause Analysis (Five Whys)
⏱ 10 minutes
▼
💡 Facilitator: The Five Whys technique often reveals systemic root causes. Encourage the team to push back — "Is that really why?"

Prompt

🎯 What to Look For

  • Do the root causes ring true, or is the AI being too generic?
  • Are there root causes the team already knows about?
  • Is the test plan realistic — do we actually have access to that data?

✏️ Team Discussion Questions

  • "Which root cause, if solved, would have the biggest cascading effect?"
  • "Do we already have the data to test these hypotheses?"
  • "What organisational barriers exist to accessing this data?"
3
AI Idea Generation & Clustering
⏱ 10 minutes
▼
💡 Facilitator: This is the creative stage. After the AI generates ideas, use dot-voting: each person gets 3 dots to vote on favourites across all clusters.

Prompt

🎯 What to Look For

  • Are ideas specific enough to be actionable?
  • Does the scoring feel right?
  • Which idea gets the team most excited?

✏️ Team Activity: Dot-Voting

  • Give each participant 3 sticky dots (or digital votes)
  • Vote on favourite ideas across all clusters
  • Tally results and discuss the winner

One-Page Pilot Brief
⏱ 10 minutes
▼
💡 Facilitator: The output should be detailed enough that a developer could build from it. Review each section critically.

Prompt

🎯 What to Look For

  • Is the user flow realistic for how this company actually works?
  • Does the sample data feel authentic?
  • Are the guardrails sufficient?
  • Would the success metrics prove value to leadership?
